About Heritage


DR. MD. MAHBUBAR RAHMAN
Founder of Heritage Archives

What is Heritage all about?

Heritage is an independent professional depository of important documents, photographs, journals and periodicals, magazines, local history books and digital items. It aims at creating a new type of open, service-oriented and user-friendly archives in Bangladesh.
Heritage has been functioning in a separate building of 1500 sqft × 3 floors. It has no permanent staff: The founder himself, few temporary staff and some volunteers work here. It remains open 4 hours a day, from 4.30 pm to 8.30 pm. Four researchers can work together. Heritage Publishes a quarterly journal titled 'Sthaniya Itihas'.

The Reason

The collective memory of Bangladesh and its 140 million inhabitants is severely threatened because there are no places where we can deposit our historical material. There are only a few archives in Bangladesh and they collect state records.
So, for collecting and preserving such materials which neither the state archives, nor the existing libraries of Bangladesh collect and preserve, ‘Heritage: Archives of Bangladesh History’ has been established.




The Focus is on:

Heritage focuses on the post-colonial period (1947 onward), but also welcomes older material. It concentrates on material produced by civil society but also include material produced by state institutions. There are 2 main focus:

1. Social movements
(pro-democracy, labour, students, peasant, women, environmental, Islamic, nationalist, human right(s), and

2. Social transformation
(emergence of NGO’s urban growth, new entrepreneurial groups, labour migration, popular culture).

Journals and Periodicals:

Heritage has already collected journals and periodicals on about 3000 titles. The periodicals are important because no library in Bangladesh collects them.
These periodicals often publish special issues on left politics, on labour movements, on fundamentalists etc.

Documents & Thesis Paper

It has rich collection of Documents on Political Parties, Student organisations, Labour organisations and women organisations.
Also, It collects M.Phil and Ph.D Thesis'. Present number is 135.

Coins & Stamps:

Heritage has collected some coins of British (1940s) and Pakistan (1960s) periods.
It has also collected all the stamps of Bangladesh from 1971-1998.

Books on Local History and Indigenous People:

The important section of Heritage is on local studies (studies on Districts and Upazilas) and on aboriginals of Bangladesh. Heritage has about 5000 books on 64 districts of Bangladesh.
It has also good number of books on the indigenous people of Bangladesh

Paper Clippings:

Heritage makes paper clipping from almost all leading dailies of Bangladesh. These have been classified into 50 different titles.

TIMELINE


  • March 2002

    Beginning of a Dream

    In March 2002, the sole initiative was the beginning of collecting different documents with a dream of establishing an archive, was started by Dr. Md. Mahbubar Rahman. Various documents in the form of leaflets, posters, souvenirs, annual or Books on local history and indigenous people and many others started piling up in his residence.
    Thus, the journey of Heritage began.

  • December 2007

    Establishment of Heritage Building

    After purchasing a land in 2006, a house was built there in December 2007 and systematic record keeping was started afterwards. The building was named as ‘Heritage Bhavan’ (Heritage building). Initially it was one stored building but with the rapid growth of collection, The bulding has been getting bigger. Currently the archives contains 3 floors.

  • June 2014

    Heritage Museum

    A Museum was established on June 2014 on the 2nd floor in order to keeping the antique collections. It contains ancient coins, papers, marriage cards, tools of war from old-bengal, gramophone etc.
    Stuffs that have become obsolate are kept in the museum.

  • NOW

    Gaining Recognition

    Researchers from all over Bangladesh are now coming here. Distinguished personalities (Ministers, Parliament members, Writers, etc.), have visited this archives. Some foreign archivist from India, Pakistan and the Netherland has visited. 'Heritage' has been a very popular place of north bengal nowadays for the researchers, historians and the students. The Dream and the vision that DR. Mahbubar Rahman had in 2002, has come to light in no time.
